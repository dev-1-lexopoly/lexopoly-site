The Master Checklist: 7 Document Types You MUST Have

EPA compliance isn't complicated‚Äîit's just specific. Here's what auditors expect to find, in order of importance:

  • Waste Manifests (Hazardous Materials) - Federal requirement for anything classified hazardous. Must show generator, transporter, and disposal facility signatures. Keep for 3 years minimum.
  • Hauler Receipts (Non-Hazardous Waste) - Proof of proper disposal for drywall, concrete, wood, metal. Include weight tickets, timestamps, facility addresses.
  • Waste Characterization Records - Lab results or visual inspection notes determining if materials are hazardous. Required before disposal.
  • Training Certificates - Proof your crew received required hazmat handling training. Document all training sessions and refresher courses.
  • Spill Response Logs - Any material release (even small ones). Note date, location, material, cleanup method, disposal.
  • Equipment Inspection Records - Proof your waste storage containers meet EPA standards (leak-proof, labeled, dated).
  • Contractor Certifications - Verify haulers and disposal facilities have current permits. Get copies annually.

Quick-Start Guide: Organizing in 30 Minutes

Don't have a system yet? Here's how to set one up fast:

Step 1: Physical Organization (10 minutes)

Get 7 file folders or binders‚Äîone for each document type above. Label them clearly. This isn't fancy, but it's findable.

Step 2: Chronological Filing (5 minutes)

Within each folder, organize by date (newest on top). This matches how EPA will ask for records: "Show me September's waste manifests."

Step 3: Digital Backup (10 minutes)

Scan or photograph your most recent 90 days of records. Store in Google Drive, Dropbox, or email them to yourself. If originals get damaged, you have backup.

Step 4: Create a Master Index (5 minutes)

One-page document listing: Folder Name ‚Üí Date Range ‚Üí Location. Tape this to your file cabinet. When the inspector asks, you know exactly where to look.

Red Flags EPA Inspectors Look For

üö® Audit Triggers That Guarantee Fines

Missing Signatures: Unsigned manifests are treated as non-existent. Penalties can reach tens of thousands of dollars per violation.

Date Gaps: If you have records for Jan-Feb and May-Jun, inspectors assume illegal disposal during March-April.

Mismatched Quantities: Manifest says 500 lbs, receipt says 350 lbs. Where'd the other 150 lbs go? Automatic investigation.

Expired Permits: Using a hauler whose permit expired 3 months ago? You're liable for their violations too.

No Characterization: Disposing of "unknown waste" is illegal. Must test or visually determine hazard level first.

Common Gaps & How to Fill Them

Gap #1: Weekend/After-Hours Disposal

Your crew dumps waste Saturday morning. No office staff to process paperwork. By Monday, the receipt is lost.

Fix: Require drivers to photograph receipts with their phone immediately. Text to office. Physical copy follows Monday.

Gap #2: Subcontractor Waste

Your electrical sub hauls their own waste. You assume they're handling documentation. EPA says it's your responsibility as general contractor.

Fix: Contract clause requiring subs to submit copies of all waste receipts within 48 hours of disposal.

Gap #3: "Miscellaneous" Materials

You have receipts labeled "mixed construction debris" with no specifics. EPA can't verify proper disposal without material details.

Fix: Train crew to specify: "drywall (non-asbestos), wood framing, metal conduit." Takes 10 extra seconds, prevents costly violations.

Hazardous materials demand the most rigorous documentation‚Äîmissing paperwork means automatic violations
